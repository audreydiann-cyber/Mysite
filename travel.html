The Case for Wandering

Why Travel Matters

Travel is one of the most powerful forms of education available to us. It collapses our assumptions, broadens our empathy, and reminds us that the world is richer and stranger than any map suggests.

Below are some of the most compelling reasons to pack your bags — organized by whether you're traveling for growth, or just for the pure joy of it.

  1. Travel for Personal Growth
    • Build genuine confidence navigating the unknown
    • Develop patience and adaptability in unfamiliar situations
    • Gain perspective on your own cultural assumptions
  2. Travel for Connection
    • Meet people whose worldview challenges and enriches yours
    • Share meals, stories, and languages across borders
    • Build friendships that transcend geography
  3. Travel for Pure Joy
    • Eat food that doesn't exist anywhere else on earth
    • Stand in front of things that genuinely take your breath away
    • Get blissfully, productively lost
Practical Wisdom

Travel Tips I Swear By

01

Pack half of what you think you need

You will always overpack. Lay everything out, then put half back. Your future self — hauling bags through cobblestone streets — will thank you.

02

Learn five phrases in the local language

Hello, thank you, please, excuse me, and "do you speak English?" — these five phrases will open doors everywhere.

03

Always have a backup of your documents

Photos of your passport, visa, insurance, and emergency contacts stored in cloud storage. Takes 10 minutes. Saves potential catastrophe.

04

Eat where the locals eat

If there's a laminated menu with photos directly outside a tourist attraction, keep walking. Follow the lunch crowds at noon.
